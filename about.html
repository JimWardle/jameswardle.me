About Me

I'm James, a DevOps Engineer based in the West Midlands. I help teams work more efficiently by automating repetitive tasks and creating documentation that people actually want to follow.

How I Work

My approach is straightforward: calm, patient, and never patronising. When I'm explaining something technical, I prefer to show rather than tell. I'll create an environment where you feel confident to try things yourself, with just enough scaffolding to support you along the way.

What drives me is turning problems into solutions. Whether that's:

  • Streamlining those repetitive tasks that eat up everyone's time
  • Writing documentation that's actually useful (not just comprehensive)
  • Turning a rough idea into a working product over a weekend
  • Helping teams ship more reliably with less stress

My Background

I didn't take a straight path to DevOps. I studied Communication and Media, spent some time teaching (which was stressful but taught me patience), and gradually found my way into infrastructure and automation.

The teaching experience, whilst challenging, gave me skills that serve me well now:

  • Breaking complex problems into manageable steps
  • Creating explanations that actually make sense
  • Staying calm when everything's on fire
  • Building confidence in others to try new approaches

What I'm Like

I tend to collect and cycle through hobbies, diving deep into something before parking it and moving on to the next interesting thing. Current and recurring interests include:

  • Board games – Strategy over luck, every time
  • 3D printing – Usually printing solutions to problems I didn't know I had
  • Tech tinkering – Modding old consoles, Raspberry Pi projects, home automation
  • Cooking and baking bread – There's something satisfying about processes that work consistently
  • Cycling and running – Helps me think through problems

My colleagues would probably tell you I have a habit of wandering off topic when discussing things (which I'd argue is just exploring interesting tangents).

Personal Life

I live with my wife, who's the Director of Operations at a cancer support charity. She's driven, funny, and has a bright personality that keeps me motivated and pushes me to stretch myself. She also gives excellent feedback on websites, as you can probably tell from this one.

Together we've worked on various side projects, and there's something amazing about turning the breadcrumb of an idea into a tangible site or product that actually works.

The bottom line: I'm someone who enjoys solving problems, explaining things clearly, and helping teams work better together. If you value calm problem-solving and practical solutions over flashy tech for its own sake, we'll probably get along well.
